Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Welcome to the exhilarating world of river rapids! The Salmon, one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States, offers diverse water conditions. In early summer, enjoy the excitement of higher, faster, and colder waters. As summer progresses, the river's flow slows down, providing a more leisurely experience with lower, slower, and warmer water. Families, especially those with children who love to swim, often prefer the late summer for its gentler, warmer river flow.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Winding through canyons in central Utah, in some of the most desolate land anywhere, the Green river flows towards the mighty Colorado river and the sea. High grey stone cliffs, beautiful desert scenery, and great rapids have made this run famous. This trip runs from from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid and boasts outstanding scenery, and great whitewater opportunities.

Embark on a breathtaking 6-day journey through Desolation Canyon, renowned as a National Landmark. This designation is a testament to the canyon's unique features and compelling historical narrative

Grand Canyon, Arizona

Experience an unforgettable river adventure with Grand Canyon river rafting, where world-class whitewater meets breathtaking scenery. Beyond the exhilarating rapids, the canyon reveals side canyons and ancient Indian ruins, accessible only by the river, adding to the richness of your journey.

Dive into the Grand Canyon's unique river rating scale, offering a comprehensive evaluation of diverse rapids and river dynamics. This scale provides insight into the varying challenges of the river journey, with a more common reference to the familiar I to V rating system for better clarity.
